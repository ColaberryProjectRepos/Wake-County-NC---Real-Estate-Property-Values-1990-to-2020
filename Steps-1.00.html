Problem Statement 1

The most populous county in North Carolina, Wake county’s real estate property values have been steadily increasing over the last 30+ years

Wake County is located in the state of North Carolina. It was the 9th fastest-growing county in the United States, with the town of Cary and the city of Raleigh being the 8th and 15th fastest-growing cities, respectively.

Wake County's population increased by 66%, 656,824 to 1,089,617, from 2001 to 2018.  During the same time, the job growth in the County grew by 44%, with 6,621 new jobs in 2020, as compared to a 13% job growth in the United States.

North Carolina is a more affordable place to live than much of the Northeast or West Coast and still has powerful ways to draw new residents, including a thriving cultural scene, a diverse population and top research universities. According to the Urban Institute, its elementary schools and middle schools perform well above average in reading and math (and notably ahead of California’s). The average price of a home in Wake county in 2020 was $283,409.  Inventory in the Raleigh market, which includes the rest of Wake County, is down 50% year-over-year and home prices have risen 14%, according to Zillow.
