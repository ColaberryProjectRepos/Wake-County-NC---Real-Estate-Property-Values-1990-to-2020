1.  Possible higher future home prices
  
For a home buyer, there is a high probability that the home prices will continue to rise.  At the current Year Over Year (YOY) increase of a home price, the average price of home will probably increase from the current average of $283,409 to $297,183.  According to U.S. News & World Report's best places to live, 2021-2022, the Raleigh and Durham metro area ranks as the 2nd (it was ranked 10th in 2019) best place to live in the United States.  It continues to state that,  "the cost of living in the Raleigh and Durham areas is roughly on par with the national average, with home prices climbing significantly over the past few years. Housing costs will continue to rise as more people flock to the area in search of a career. However, relatively low property taxes make home ownership here easier than in some other major cities." 

2. Sellers  market

For a home owner who wants to sell his/her house, this is probably a good time to sell as their home value has probably increased over the year(s).  As of June 2021, according to Fonville Morisey Realty, a Long & Foster ® Real Estate Company, the largest privately owned real estate company in the nation, the median sale price for a home in Wake county was $329,000.  The total of active inventory was 860 units as compared to 3,143 units the same period a year ago.  Homes have also been staying on the market for a shorter period as compared to a year ago.  Homes in Raleigh, Wake County's largest township, are typically selling in just four days, according to Zillow, which ranks the metro area as second in the U.S. for the fewest days a home is officially listed on the market.  Nicole Bachaud, economic data analyst at Zillow, says that though around 44% of homes are selling above list price in the Raleigh metro area, the majority of homes sold didn’t, so buyers still have a chance at more affordable houses.
